Popular Filipino Dishes

Adobo

A savory dish made with chicken or pork marinated in vinegar, soy sauce, and garlic.

Calories: 280 | Protein: 25g | Carbs: 5g | Fat: 18g

★ ★ ★ ★ ★
  • Step 1: Marinate chicken or pork in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, pepper, and bay leaves for at least 30 minutes.
  • Step 2: Heat oil in a pan and sauté garlic and onions until fragrant.
  • Step 3: Add the marinated meat and cook until lightly browned.
  • Step 4: Pour in the marinade and add water. Bring to a boil.
  • Step 5: Simmer on low heat until the meat is tender and the sauce reduces. Adjust seasoning to taste.
  • Step 6: Serve hot with steamed rice.

Sinigang

A sour soup made with tamarind, tomatoes, and various vegetables, often with pork or shrimp.

Calories: 220 | Protein: 22g | Carbs: 10g | Fat: 9g

★ ★ ★ ★ ★
  • Step 1: In a pot, bring water to a boil and dissolve tamarind base or extract.
  • Step 2: Add pork ribs or shrimp and cook until tender.
  • Step 3: Add tomatoes, onions, and radish slices. Let them cook for 5 minutes.
  • Step 4: Add okra, string beans, and kangkong (water spinach). Simmer for another 5-10 minutes.
  • Step 5: Adjust the sourness and seasoning as needed. Serve hot.

Kare-Kare

A savory peanut-based stew made with oxtail and vegetables.

Calories: 350 | Protein: 18g | Carbs: 50g | Fat: 10g

★ ★ ★ ★ ★
  • Step 1: Boil oxtail, tripe, or beef in water until tender. Set the broth aside.
  • Step 2: Sauté garlic and onions in a pot. Add the boiled meat.
  • Step 3: Stir in peanut butter and annatto powder for color. Add the broth to create a thick sauce.
  • Step 4: Add eggplant, banana blossoms, and string beans. Cook until tender.
  • Step 5: Serve hot with shrimp paste (bagoong) on the side.

Lechon

Roasted whole pig, known for its crispy skin and tender meat.

Calories: 270 kcal | Protein: 24g | Carbs: 0g | Fat: 20g

★ ★ ★ ★ ★
  • Step 1: Clean the pig thoroughly. Rub salt and pepper inside and out.
  • Step 2: Stuff the pig with lemongrass, garlic, onions, and bay leaves.
  • Step 3: Sew the pig's belly shut and marinate overnight.
  • Step 4: Roast over charcoal while basting with oil to achieve crispy skin.
  • Step 5: Once golden brown, carve and serve with liver sauce or lechon gravy.

Halo-Halo

A popular dessert made with crushed ice, sweet beans, fruits, and leche flan.

Calories: 450 kcal | Protein: 8g | Carbs: 95g | Fat: 10g

★ ★ ★ ★ ★
  • Step 1: Prepare ingredients like sweetened beans, nata de coco, jackfruit, and ube halaya.
  • Step 2: In a glass, layer the ingredients and add crushed ice.
  • Step 3: Pour evaporated milk over the ice.
  • Step 4: Top with leche flan, purple yam, and a scoop of ice cream. Serve immediately.

Pancit

Stir-fried noodles with vegetables, meat, and seafood, often served during birthdays.

Calories: 300 kcal | Protein: 12g | Carbs: 45g | Fat: 8g

★ ★ ★ ★ ★
  • Step 1: Soak dried noodles in water until soft. Drain.
  • Step 2: Sauté garlic and onions in oil. Add chicken, pork, or shrimp and cook until done.
  • Step 3: Add vegetables like carrots, cabbage, and celery. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes.
  • Step 4: Add noodles and seasoning (soy sauce and fish sauce). Mix well.
  • Step 5: Serve with lemon or calamansi slices on the side.

Bibingka

A rice cake made with coconut milk, often topped with salted egg and cheese.

Calories: 320 kcal | Protein: 6g | Carbs: 55g | Fat: 9g

★ ★ ★ ★ ★
  • Step 1: Combine rice flour, sugar, baking powder, and coconut milk to form a batter.
  • Step 2: Line molds with banana leaves. Pour the batter into the molds.
  • Step 3: Top with slices of salted egg and grated cheese.
  • Step 4: Bake in a preheated oven until golden brown.
  • Step 5: Serve warm with butter and grated coconut on top.

Lumpia

Filipino spring rolls filled with vegetables and meat, often served with a dipping sauce.

Calories: 120 kcal | Protein: 5g | Carbs: 10g | Fat: 7g

★ ★ ★ ★ ★
  • Step 1: Sauté ground pork, carrots, cabbage, and onions in a pan.
  • Step 2: Let the filling cool, then wrap it in lumpia wrappers tightly.
  • Step 3: Heat oil in a frying pan and deep fry the rolls until golden brown.
  • Step 4: Drain excess oil and serve with sweet chili sauce or vinegar dip.
